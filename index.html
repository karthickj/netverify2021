NetVerify 2021- Network Verification Workshop

In conjunction with the 29th IEEE ICNP 2021, Dallas, USA, November 2-5, 2021

 

Overview

NetVerify brings together researchers and practitioners working on verifying the reliability, trustworthiness, and dependability of computer networks. It seeks to create an interdisciplinary forum to foster interactions between researchers/practitioners from fields such as networking, security, formal methods, programming languages, and machine learning.

Topics of Interest

We invite talk proposals on early stage ideas, position papers, and case studies on all topics relating to verifying reliability, dependability, and trustworthiness of computer networks including, but not limited to: 

1.     Automated verification and testing of all aspects of computer networks

2.     Algorithms for verifying control plane and data plane

3.     Managing, debugging, and diagnosing problems in networked systems

4.     Network configuration synthesis

5.     Languages for networking and security

6.     Case studies of deploying verification tools and methods

7.     Machine learning driven failure prediction and recovery

 

Format

The workshop aims to stimulate discussions between researchers and practitioners in all fields working on assuring the reliability of computer networks. The format will comprise a combination of invited and contributed talks, and a panel discussion. The speaker may talk about previously early stage ideas, positions, and case studies from deploying verification techniques.  Participation in the workshop is open to everyone. To give a talk, please submit a 1-2 page description of the proposed talk; these will be reviewed by the workshop co-chairs.
